## Legged robot gaits

<div style="text-align: center; width: 100%;">
    <div style="display: inline-block; width: 24%; vertical-align: top; text-align: center;">
        <img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/DARoSLab/EAGLE/main/assets/img/gait/trot.gif" style="width: 100%; vertical-align: top;"/>
        <p>Trot gait involves diagonal legs moving together, offering a mix of speed and stability and producing clear, high-quality RGB images and point cloud data.</p>
    </div>
    <div style="display: inline-block; width: 24%; vertical-align: top; text-align: center;">
        <img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/DARoSLab/EAGLE/main/assets/img/gait/bounding.gif" style="width: 100%; vertical-align: top;"/>
        <p>Bounding gait propels forward with front and rear legs moving together, enabling rapid angular velocities.</p>
    </div>
    <div style="display: inline-block; width: 24%; vertical-align: top; text-align: center;">
        <img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/DARoSLab/EAGLE/main/assets/img/gait/pronking.gif" style="width: 100%; vertical-align: top;"/>
        <p>Pronking gait sees all four legs moving in unison, presenting high linear velocities.</p>
    </div>
    <div style="display: inline-block; width: 24%; vertical-align: top; text-align: center;">
        <img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/DARoSLab/EAGLE/main/assets/img/gait/backflip.gif" style="width: 100%; vertical-align: top;"/>
        <p>Backflips demonstrate agility with rotations up to $750~\si{\degree/\second}$, requiring precise control and strong actuation, mainly for acrobatics and showcasing athletic ability.</p>
